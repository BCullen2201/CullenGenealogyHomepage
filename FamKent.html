Descendants of Lord Richard Cullen
of Sittingbourn, Kent, England
forwarded by John & Dick Cullen
additional information provided by Michael B. Cullen
There are several sources of information on the Cullen families from Kent in England. It is clear that Cullens have been present there for many generations prior to the earliest recorded individual in the genealogy on this page. The earliest recorded individual, Richard Cullen who died about 1850, is the subject of ongoing research for several interested groups. My own interest is in the suspicion that there is some connection between the Cullens families related to Lord Richard Cullen of Kent and my own family line in Upton, Nottinghamshire.

I will point out here that my site is not the only reference to the Cullen families outlined in the genealogy below. There are several other websites containing information more complete and useful than the summary which I have included here. To help you along, I have collected the links that you may find helpful. They are:

  • Cullen Web Page: Pat and John Cullen's website which includes a genealogy on the descendants of Lord Richard Cullen of Kent, England.
  • Johnnie Wayne Brown's Home Page: A retired electrical engineer who's genealogy research includes the same Cullen family. He has a large page on his site outlining all known descendants of Lord Richard Cullen. His information comes from the book "Cullen Genealogy" by George Lee Cullen.

Before going into the family data, I would like to pass on some of the oral tradition concerning the ancestry of this Richard Cullen. Much of this information came from correspondence with a contact in England, discovered through a reference in the Family Registry File at the LDS Library. Michael Cullen provided these details from his and other's research and knows more than we are able to present here from the few letters still held by the family. I have put together the bits and pieces into one story as best as I was able to:

In 1066, there was a Culling that came over with William the Conqueror from Normandy and, for his valiant service in the Battle of Hastings, Culling was awarded his peerage and made Lord at Devon. It is the descendants of these Cullen/Culling Lords of the Manor at Devon, Devonshire, that Michael Cullen felt we were certainly related to. In Kent, the Cullen surname can be traced back to the 14'th century. As for my line in Upton, Notts., spelling variations included: Cullen, Cullin, Culling, & Cullinge. Concerning the spelling variations in the Nottinghamshire Cullens, I am told that the difference between the Cullin and Cullen families is marked, especially in Lincolnshire. In some graveyards, one finds both spellings. It is believed that the Cullens came down from Scotland while the Cullin families came up from Norfolk, losing the 'G' on the way; thus, Culling became Cullin. This rule is not hard and fast since spelling variations occur on both sides. - J.C. Among the descendants of the Cullen/Culling Lords of Devon, there is given one Lord Richard of Kent, said to be the son of Lady Gray, Queen of England. Evidently, Richard took as his surname the maiden name of his mother and so we then have Lord Richard Cullen. This occured several generations prior to the Lord Richard Cullen that begins the genealogy on this page below. That this earlier Lord Richard was the son of Lady Jane Gray, short lived Queen of England, is something of a debate that is ongoing. Another question is the title "Lord" which has been applied here. It seems this was added later, just as the "Queen of England" was likely added at a later time concerning the connection to the Grey family. There are other Gray/Grey (or de Grey) families that also have ties to the Cullen family. Either way, it is pointless to rehash the details of the debate; future research will hopefully clear up the apparent contradiction. For now, we begin the genealogy as we know it; descendants of Lord Richard Cullen who died about 1850 in Sittingbourn, Kent, England. This family tree has been updated with new information from Michael Cullen who has been located at long last. As is customary for all genealogies on my site, I have not included information on individuals in later generations for reasons of privacy. I have all the individuals in four generations only, for posting to this page.

Though the format of the family tree below can be difficult to follow, it allows me to make updates rather easily and all the information is present with minimum fuss. It may be best to search the page for names using your browser's search function. When the download section of this site is completed, the GEDCOM will be freely available.

Concerning Richard Cullen who died in 1850 at Sittingbourne. According to the information gathered from his will, Richard lived at Greenstreet Farm in Teynham. He was baptised in 1762, a son of Robert & Ann Cullen. Additionally, there were these brothers and sisters of Richard:

  • Mary: christened 1752 at Lynsted.
  • John: christened 1753 at Lynsted.
  • Robert: christened 1755 at Lynsted.
  • William: christened 1760 at Lynsted.
  • Thomas: christened 1765 at Lynsted.
  • Hetty Bella: christened 1767 at Lynsted.
