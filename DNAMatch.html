This first table is the result of a search at Ysearch using my haplotype which I will refer to as the Cullens of Upton haplotype. I restricted my search by only comparing those who have at least 25-marker haplotype in the database. I have neglected to include YSearch ID for reasons of privacy since these tables are used for my research only. There are no exact matches of course, so I allowed a one-step mismatch for every marker above sixteen. Our closest matches are then at a genetic distance of seven. The coloring of the Markers is based on mutation rate; red is very fast, light red is fast, white is around average, light blue is slow, dark blue is very slow. The rates vary depending on several factors. The first two entries are the two variations on the "I1b2*-A" haplogroup, and the third entry is my haplotype. Most of my closest genetic matches are indeed of the "I1b2*-A" haplogroup. Note here that Ken discussses two varieties based on the value at DYS448 being 19 or 21 for varieties A or B. Ken is aware that there are those with DYS448=20 for a possible C variety. Well, I now see results with DYS448 at 18 repeats; a fourth variety. My only unusual value is my 18 repeats at DYS385b.

This second table is just a test of a suspicion that perhaps my DYS464a-d was just a touch unusual. YSearch throws a wrench into the works, making it a bit difficult as far as searching goes; you must include at least eight markers if you manually specify a haplotype. I've tried to make the best of it though and it's really not that bad since you do want to specify variations and there are enough modal values that are pretty much universal to make up the rest of the eight markers required for a search. At any rate, I've chosen here the modal 8,10,10,12 at DYS459a,b,455,454 along with my "suspicious" DYS464a-d. DYS464 is a fast marker, true but, it still exhibits usual and unusual behavior! Indeed, the search result indicates that the majority of the haplotypes that match the markers are of the "I1b2*-B" variety. I'm one of the few "I1b2*-A" folks that does match the 14,15,15,15 repeats at DYS464a-d. Another oddity that pops up is my 29 repeats at DYS389-2. While 29 is not really unusual at all for this marker, it sure seems to be unusual for those with the 14,15,15,15 repeats at DYS464a-d. The usual value would then be 28 repeats for DYS389-2.
