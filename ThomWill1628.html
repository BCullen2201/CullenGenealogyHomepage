Will of Thomas Cullen, Yeoman of Upton
Written Jul 25, 1628, Probate Granted Sep 23, 1628
Jim Cullen

Thomas Cullen was a Yeoman and Churchwarden of Upton, Nottinghamshire and was born c.1573. His first wife was Agnes Kitchen and he married again to Susan Saman in 1611. According to the will transcribed below, he was married a third time and her name was Elizabeth. Thomas was Churchwarden at St Peter & St Paul's Church on Church Lane in Upton from 1600-1625 and he oversaw many repairs to the church in the early years of his term as Churchwarden.


The Will of Thomas Cullen, Yeoman and Churchwarden of Upton, Nottinghamshire
Written Jul 25, 1628 & Probate Granted Sep 23, 1628


Cullen Cullyn Thomas yeoman Upton W p Sept 23, 1628 Will [2] Inv. Bond Not reg[ister]ed Coram Mr Holder 23 Septemb[e]r 1628 Test[amentum] Tho: Cullyn

In the name of god Amen the five and twentieth daye of July Anno d[omi]ni 1628 I Thomas Cullen of Upton in the countie of Nott[ingham] yeom[an] beinge sicke in body but of good and p[er]fect memorie thankes be given to Allmightie god doe make this my last will & testam[en]t in manner & forme followinge First I bequeath my soule into the handes of Allmightie god my maker and redeemer and my body to the earth First I give and bequeath unto George Cullin my sonn the some of tenn poundes of lawfull mony of England to be payed unto him within one whole yeare next after my decease Item I give & bequeath unto Sara Cullin my daughter the some of fortie poundes of lawfull mony of England to be payed unto hir when shee attaine to the age of one & twentie yeares Item I give and bequeath to John Cullyn my sonn the some of fiftie poundes of lawfull mony of England and one silver salte a p[in]t and a beaker and all my satisfaction of silver to be payed and delivered unto him when he shall attane to the age of twentie & one yeares Item I give & bequeath to William Cullen my sonn the some of fortie poundes of lawfull mony of England in full satisfaction of all his childes p[ar]t to be payed unto him when he attaine to the age of twentie and one yeares Item I give and bequeath unto Thomas Cullen my sonn the some of five poundes of lawfull mony of England in full satisfaction of his childes p[ar]te to be payed unto him when he attaine to the age of twentie and one yeares Item I give and bequeath to George [?] his childeren five poundes of lawfull mony of England in full satisfaction of their childes p[ar]ts to be payed when they shall attaine to the age of twentie and one yeares Item I give and bequeath to Richard Cullyn my sonn the some of tenne poundes of lawfull mony of England in full satisfaction of his childes p[ar]te to be payed unto him when he shall attaine to the age of twentie and one yeares Item I give and bequeath to Elizabeth Cullen my wife the land in Morton and all the p[re]misses thereunto belonginge for and duringe hir naturall life and after hir decease I give and bequeath the same land in Morton w[i]th all and singuler the p[re]misses thereunto belonginge to John Cullen my sonn and his heires for ever and for want of such heires to the right heires of me the said Thomas Cullen for evermore all the rest of my goodes cattells and chattells whatsoev[er] I give and bequeath to Elizabeth my said wife whome I make & ordaine sole executrix of this my last will & testam[en]t Witnesses Thomas [Barker?] William [Horspoole?] John Mason George Wright jurat[us] Thomas Cullyn his marke +
